Leaving my hometown of New Orleans for a minute....
to travel and work remotely around the world.

As many of you know, I've been accepted to the 2nd class of the Remote Year program. In February, I will join a community of 75 other 'remotes' to travel through South America, Eastern Europe and Southeast Asia. We will travel together, to 12 different cities each month, mastering the challenges of remote work, enjoying new locations and cultures, and hopefully collaborating with and fostering the growing international community of 'digital nomads.'

Fucking millennials.

Phase 1: South America

Montevideo, Uruguay

First stop out of the gate is the the costal capital city of Uruguay. It is here I wlll meet my Remote Year crew living in the center of the city and working out of the Sinergia Cowork space.

  • February

Buenos Aires, Argentina

The following month we take a ferry across the Río de la Plata and settle in apartments in Palermo. We have two La Maquinita co-working spaces available to us within walking distance.

  • March

La Paz, Bolivia

Levaing the larger cities behind, we will then head to the elevation of La Paz, Bolivia.
"At 12,000 ft elevation, and colder weather, La Paz will definitely take a toll on those doing a lot of walking or drinking.    Uh oh.

  • April

Cusco, Peru

A scenic city regarded as the capital and birthplace of the Inca empire, this historic, booming destination in the Peruvian Andes will host us in its most picturesque neighborhoods. Looking forward to learning more about this Túpac I've heard so much about.
